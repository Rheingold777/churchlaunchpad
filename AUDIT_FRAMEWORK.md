# ChurchLaunchpad Audit Framework

The 4 pillars every church assessment checks. Each pillar rolls up specific signals. This is the template we reuse for every prototype church going forward.

---

## Pillar 1 — Findability

Can the right people discover this church?

**Signals:**
- Google Business Profile claimed? Completeness score?
- Service times, address, phone visible on GBP?
- Real photos (not stock, not logo only)?
- Church category set correctly (e.g. Pentecostal Church, Baptist Church)?
- Appears in map pack for "church near me" + city?
- Appears in top 10 for "[city] [denomination] church"?
- Sitemap.xml exists and matches actual pages?
- Page titles include church name + city?
- Meta descriptions present, not truncated?
- Robots.txt allows indexing?
- HTTPS enforced site-wide?

**Why it matters:** Visibility is the top of the funnel. A church can be healthy and still grow slowly if strangers can't find it.

---

## Pillar 2 — First Impression

A stranger lands on the homepage. Do they know what to do in 5 seconds?

**Signals:**
- Church name visible and readable?
- Tagline or mission statement present and clear?
- Service times in the top half of the page, not buried?
- Physical address visible, or a clear "Plan Your Visit" path?
- Hero image is real (a photo of this church), not a template placeholder?
- Mobile layout works — service times still visible on a phone?
- Page loads under 3 seconds?
- Typography and colors feel intentional, not default?
- No typos in hero copy or nav?

**Why it matters:** Visitors decide in seconds whether to stay or bounce. A strong first impression turns Google traffic into actual visits.

---

## Pillar 3 — Trust

Does this church feel real, current, and credible online?

**Signals:**
- Copyright year matches current year (stale years = red flag)?
- Default template labels cleaned up (e.g. no "| Mysite", no "Screen Shot 2022-.png" as logo)?
- Old event pages retired (nothing promoting past events)?
- Photos look current, not from 5+ years ago?
- Contact info resolves — email goes to a real inbox, phone works, form submits?
- Church has a working domain email (pastor@thischurch.com, not personal Gmail)?
- Website and social profiles match each other (name, logo, description)?
- Pastor or leadership is named and visible (About page)?
- Reviews exist on Google and are generally positive?
- HTTPS lock shown, no security warnings?

**Why it matters:** Trust is built or lost before a visitor ever walks in. Small cues accumulate into "this church is alive and well-run" or "this looks abandoned."

---

## Pillar 4 — Next Step

Can a curious visitor take action easily?

**Signals:**
- "Plan Your Visit" or "I'm New" button visible above the fold?
- Clear answer to "what should I expect"?
- What to wear / where to park / when to arrive — covered?
- Prayer request or contact form works and gets replied to?
- Small group / ministry / baptism paths clear for people who want more?
- Kids check-in process explained (huge for families)?
- Link to giving if someone wants to give?
- Link to online sermons if someone wants to watch before visiting?
- Newsletter or weekly recap they can subscribe to?
- Every link on the homepage works?

**Why it matters:** Online discovery only pays off if there's a clear on-ramp to real life. Most churches nail the message and miss this.

---

## How to score

Each pillar gets a pass/concern/fail rating. A church doesn't need 100% — they need enough strengths per pillar to feel credible, and zero glaring failures.

**Report format to the pastor:**
- 3 strengths (what's working — build confidence)
- 3 specific findings (what stood out — earns credibility)
- 1 clear next step (reply for 3 priority recommendations)

**Three-recs follow-up covers:**
- 1 high-leverage Findability fix (usually GBP)
- 1 Trust or First Impression quick win
- 1 next-step fix that ties discovery to a visit

## Reusable wins by pillar

Things that show up at almost every church. Speeds up writing findings emails.

**Findability**
- GBP unclaimed or half-filled
- Category wrong or missing
- Page titles use default CMS labels (Wix "| Mysite", Squarespace boilerplate)
- Meta descriptions missing on half the pages

**First Impression**
- Stock or template hero image
- Service times not in top half on mobile
- Typos in hero, tagline, or nav
- Address hidden behind an extra click

**Trust**
- Stale copyright year
- Old event pages live in search results
- Contact form goes to dead inbox
- No working church-domain email (pastor uses personal Gmail)
- Instagram exists but isn't linked from the site

**Next Step**
- No "Plan Your Visit" block anywhere
- Kids ministry info absent or hidden
- Directions link just opens the browser's geolocation, no embedded map
- No link from social back to website
